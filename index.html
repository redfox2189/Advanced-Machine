History of Robotic Arms

Robotic arms have a rich history that dates back to the mid-20th century. The first industrial robot, known as Unimate, was developed in the 1950s by George Devol and Joseph Engelberger. Unimate was designed to perform repetitive tasks in manufacturing, such as welding and material handling. Over the years, robotic arms have evolved significantly, incorporating advanced technologies like computer vision, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. Today, robotic arms are widely used in various industries, including automotive, electronics, healthcare, and logistics, enhancing productivity and precision in tasks ranging from assembly to surgery.

SCARA Robots

SCARA (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm) robots are a type of robotic arm designed for high-speed and precise assembly tasks. They are characterized by their horizontal movement and rigid vertical axis, allowing them to perform tasks such as pick-and-place operations, assembly, and packaging with great accuracy. SCARA robots are commonly used in industries like electronics manufacturing, automotive assembly, and pharmaceuticals due to their ability to handle delicate components while maintaining speed and efficiency.
