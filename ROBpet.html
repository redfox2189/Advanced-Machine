Robotic Pets

History of Robotic Pets

The first known robotic pet was a robot dog called Sparko, built by the American company Westinghouse in 1940. It never got sold due to poor public interest. The first robotic pets to be put on the market were Hasbro's Furby in 1998 and Sony's AIBO in 1999. Since then, robotic pets have grown increasingly advanced.

Unitree B2W

The Unitree B2W is an advanced robotic pet designed to resemble a dog, developed by Unitree Robotics. It features a quadruped design that allows it to move with agility and stability, making it capable of navigating various terrains. The B2W is equipped with multiple sensors, including cameras and LIDAR, enabling it to perceive its environment and interact with users. It can perform a range of behaviors, such as walking, running, sitting, and responding to voice commands. The Unitree B2W is often used for research, education, and entertainment purposes, showcasing the potential of robotic pets in providing companionship and assistance in daily life.
